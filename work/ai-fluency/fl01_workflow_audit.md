# FL-01: Workflow Audit

## My Recurring Tasks

| # | Task | Classification | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Understanding a new ML/math concept for class | Collaborate with AI | AI can explain concepts in different ways until it clicks, but I need to actually engage to understand it, not just read the answer |
| 2 | Debugging Python/C++ code errors | Delegate to AI with review | AI often spots the bug fast, but I check the fix actually works and understand why |
| 3 | Writing code from scratch for assignments | Collaborate with AI | I want to understand the logic myself, so I write with AI as a helper, not let it do everything |
| 4 | Running the ML internship notebooks (Colab) | Just me | I need to actually run and observe results myself, that's the whole point of the exercise |
| 5 | Interpreting notebook output / discovery findings | Collaborate with AI | AI helps me understand what a number means, but the interpretation and write-up should be mine |
| 6 | Writing markdown reports (research question framing, etc.) | Delegate to AI with review | AI can draft it based on my input, but I review and adjust so it reflects my actual thinking |
| 7 | Fixing GitHub/Colab save issues | Delegate to AI with review | AI is fast at troubleshooting tool problems; I just need to follow the steps correctly |
| 8 | Deciding which capstone lane/project to pursue | Just me | This is my own judgment call about what I'm interested in and capable of, AI can inform it, not decide it |
| 9 | Writing web development code (HTML/CSS/JS projects) | Collaborate with AI | Faster to build with AI's help, but I still need to understand and adjust the code myself |
| 10 | Reviewing/proofreading my CV, LinkedIn, or written work | Delegate to AI with review | AI catches wording issues fast; I make the final call on how I want to present myself |
| 11 | Studying/memorizing for exams | Just me | Actual understanding and retention has to come from me, AI can't take the test for me |
| 12 | Summarizing long documentation/guides (like this repo's docs) | Fully automate | Low-risk, just needs a quick accurate summary so I don't have to read everything word for word |
| 13 | Formatting/organizing repetitive files (e.g. converting notes to Markdown) | Fully automate | Purely mechanical work, no judgment needed |

## My 3 Target Tasks (for FL-02 through FL-04)

1. **Debugging Python/C++ code errors**
   Success = I can explain what caused the bug in my own words, not just paste a working fix. My time to resolve a bug gets shorter over the next few weeks compared to solving it alone.

2. **Writing markdown reports (research question framing, assignment write-ups)**
   Success = the final report reflects my own reasoning and findings, not generic AI text, and passes the assignment's grading criteria on the first submission.

3. **Interpreting notebook output / discovery findings**
   Success = I can explain what a metric (like Precision@50 or a correlation number) means and why it matters, without needing AI to re-explain it the next time I see it.
